The Cedar Cultural Center

416 Cedar Avenue

Minneapolis, MN 55454

The Cedar is a very chill venue. So chill that it is staffed by volunteers, how chill is that, people just work there for free. The space is a little bit like standing in a highschool gym but we don't hold that against them. As a short and perpetually tired person, the Cedar's seated shows are my favorite thing since staying home and watching nextflix.

Mill City Nights

111 North 5th St

Minneapolis, MN 55403

Booooooooooo, Mill City Nights is owned by AEG Live (Anschutz Entertainment Group) the bajillion dollar LA corporation that also runs such venues as the Target Center (charming). Basically Mill City Nights is their "down-home" rock club, competing with locally-owned business. Also, funny story, when Mill City Nights opened in 2012 it was called The Brick. The opening night with Janes Addiction was such a sucky disaster (rude staff, epic bathroom lines, minimal beer selection, fire-hazard-sized crowds, shitty sight-lines) that they had to close down, refund all tickets and completely rebrand!

Acadia Cafe

329 Cedar Avenue

Minneapolis, MN 55454

The few times I have been to Acadia it is half full of people actually trying to watch whatever band is jammed in the corner and half full of U of M student getting drunk, loudly. I'm not a one to shush people, but this acoustically challenged space brings out the worst in me. They have good fries though.
